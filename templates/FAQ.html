Frequently Asked Questions


Is there an easy way to pick out parts for my PC?

There are plenty of websites out there that let you pick out parts for your PC, but my favorite is PC part picker. When you pick out your parts, it takes into account if the parts are compatible with each other and even provides a wattage estimate to help you decide on which power supply you should choose.

How much RAM do I need?

That depends on what you intend to use your machine for. For general office use you can get away with only having 8 GB of RAM. For gaming, a minimum of 16GB is recommended to keep up with most modern games. If you plan to do more than just gaming, and multi-task a lot, you may want to consider 32GB of RAM.

What are the price points for gaming rigs?

Price points can vary depending on the parts you get and if you're willing to sacrifice spending less on some parts to be able to spend more on others, but here is a rough estimate of what you'll need at each level based on graphics settings and resolutions.

  • Budget(1080p high settings): $700-$800
  • 1080p ultra settings: $1000-$1200
  • 1440p ultra settings: $1500

Anything higher than $1500 can generally run just about any game with the highest settings and resolutions

Am I better off getting a pre-built machine?

Depending on how you plan to use your machine, it can be more beneficial to just buy a pre-built PC. Unless you intend to use your computer for more than just office use, or you want to learn how to build a computer, you normally would probably just want to buy a PC that is already put together. Even for gaming, there are plenty of high quality pre-built machines out there ready for use right out of the box. Generally, if you're interested in learning about how to build a PC, wish to have more control over what parts go into the PC, or you plan to build your PC to upgrade in the future, that's when you'd want to build your own machine.

What kind of peripherals do I need?

For every PC, you generally want to have a monitor, a mouse, a keyboard, and some way to output sound which can either be speakers or headphones. Some monitors come with speakers built in already, so that may be something you want to consider.

What kind of monitor should I get for gaming?

For gaming, you generally want a monitor with a high refresh rate and high resolution. For an intro to gaming with a low price you can get away with a 1080p monitor with a 160hz refresh rate and still get good visuals. If you want to have a better visual experience, a monitor with a 1440p resolution and 240hz is an excellent place to start. The best visual experience would be a monitor with 4K resolution and refresh rate of 240hz or more.
